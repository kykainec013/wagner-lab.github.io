The Gifford Weary Social Psychology Symposium

I:Advances in Motivation and Self-Regulation

The social psychology program at The Ohio State University is pleased to announce the Gifford Weary Social Psychology Symposia a series of symposia to be held annually over the next four years in Columbus, Ohio. We are grateful to long-time faculty member and now professor emeritus, Dr. Gifford Weary, whose generous gift made this symposia series possible.

The first of these symposia will be held on Monday, May 22, 2017, and will focus on the topic of "Advances in Motivation and Self-Regulation". The one-day program will include talks by Elliot Berkman (Oregon), Grainne Fitzsimons (Duke), Abigail Scholer (Waterloo), and Yaacov Trope (NYU). There will also be shorter talks by local OSU social psychology faculty.

Apply to give a blitz talks

We are also soliciting abstracts for brief blitz talk presentations by advanced graduate students and post-doctoral researchers at other institutions. Thanks to Dr. Weary's generosity, we will be able to reimburse all blitz presenters' travel and hotel expenses. The deadline for submissions will be Friday, March 31, 2017. If interested, please submit the following information to fujita.5@osu.edu:

  • Name
  • Affiliation
  • Rank (what year in PhD program/post-doc position)
  • Talk title
  • Abstract (150 words maximum)
  • CV
  • Contact information for a reference (i.e., research advisor).

All blitz talk presentation decisions will be made by Friday, April 14, 2017.
